<Section icon={Map} title="The Mermaid Mask steam: Demo Walkthrough">

The coastal town of Stonetop is shrouded in thick fog, rotting wood, and forgotten secrets. In the demo of **The Mermaid Mask steam** version, players assume the role of an investigator arriving at the harbor in the dead of night. Alongside your sleeping assistant, Sally, you are tasked with unraveling a baffling locked-room murder that occurred deep within a newly surfaced submarine. 

This walkthrough covers the essential progression paths, hidden details, and environmental puzzles you will encounter while exploring the harbor and the mysterious vessel.

**Video Highlights:**
- **Stonetop Inn Investigation**: Finding the initial case file and historical lore books at 4:00 AM.
- **Harbor Navigation**: Tracking the eerie green light past the fountain square to the docked submarine.
- **Submarine Exploration**: Navigating the pink-lit drawing room and locating the locked serpent cabinet.
- **The Illusion Theater**: Examining the final crime scene, analyzing the victim, and inspecting the mysterious cauldron.

Your journey begins inside the Stonetop Inn. The atmosphere is immediately hostile and abandoned. While Sally rests on a bench, you must scour the tavern for clues. The wood is rotting, the bar stools are unstable, and a single book titled *Hex of the Helmsman* by JD Worman sits on the counter. This book details historical fishing trips onboard a ship named the *Dearest Selena*, hinting at the deep-seated maritime lore of the region.

On the bar counter, you will find the official case file left by Captain Seafoam, the mysterious figure who hired you. The file outlines the bizarre death of Magnus Mortuga, but the captain is nowhere to be found. Suddenly, an eerie green light shines through the windows from the harbor, prompting you to wake Sally and venture out into the cold night.

<Callout type="warning" title="Environmental Hazard">
Do not waste time trying to interact with the rotting bar stools or locked doors inside the inn. Your primary objective is to secure the case file and follow the green light outside before it fades.
</Callout>

Navigating the harbor requires a keen eye for detail. The path to the submarine is littered with remnants of Stonetop's glory days, including the abandoned Mr. Grip Emporium and a decaying billboard advertising a performance by Whan Wolf at the Fortuna Hall theater. 

To reach the crime scene and begin your official deduction, follow these precise steps.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Examine the Stonetop Fountain">
    Locate the large stone statue in the center of the square. It depicts a fisherman wrestling with a creature known as the Silkworm Serpent. Read the inscription to understand the local mythology surrounding sea serpents with legs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Locate the Mortuga Submarine">
    Follow the fading green light to the edge of the harbor. You will discover a surfaced submarine. Note the nameplate on the hull: the original name *Seafo* has been aggressively crossed out and replaced with *Mortuga*, signaling a past mutiny.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Enter the Submarine Hatch">
    Climb down the main ladder into the dark interior. The control room door is heavily taped and locked from the inside, with a warning note instructing you to proceed directly to the back of the vessel to locate the crime scene.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Secure the Drawing Room Clues">
    Before heading to the crime scene, explore the drawing room. Turn on the main light switch to illuminate the pink-hued room. Inspect the puffer fish lamp, the playing cards, the half-finished wine glass, and the locked serpent statue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Submarine Puzzles and Clue Solutions">

The interior of the *Mortuga* is designed to disorient and intrigue. As you move past the locked control room, you enter a lavishly decorated drawing room. The room plays unsettling, classical music through wall-mounted speakers, evoking the atmosphere of a masquerade party. 

To progress further, you must analyze the environmental anomalies and prepare to solve the mechanical puzzles left behind by the crew.

One of the central puzzles in this area involves a large metal statue of a serpent with a built-in cabinet. This cabinet is secured by a locking mechanism consisting of four rotating dials, each displaying different shapes in a central window. To unlock it, you must cross-reference the shapes with details found in the *Hex of the Helmsman* book and the physical layout of the drawing room's Z-shaped (or S-shaped) tables.

| Clue Item | Description | Investigation Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Wooden Stool | Overturned standard bar stool | Matches the Stonetop Inn stools, suggesting transport |
| Serpent Cabinet | Metal statue with four rotating shape dials | Requires a specific combination to unlock the interior |
| Cauldron | Locked metal vessel at Mortuga's feet | Scratched on the inside, suggesting something was trapped |
| Upside-Down Poster | Taped drawing of the Illusion Theater | Possible misdirection or symbolic clue left by the killer |
| Broken Bottle | Shattered glass on the theater floor | Dropped by Mortuga during the fatal struggle |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Analyzing the Illusion Theater Crime Scene">

At the very back of the submarine corridor lies the "Illusion Theater." This tiny space, separated from the corridor by a simple door, is where Magnus Mortuga met his end. The crime scene presents a classic locked-room mystery that challenges your logical deduction skills.

Magnus Mortuga was found lying on his back with a massive cut across his neck. Strangely, there is no murder weapon at the scene—no knife, blade, or sharp tool. The room was bolted securely from the inside, leaving no logical exit route for a human killer. 

To piece together what happened, you must weigh the three primary theories supported by the environmental evidence.

However, close inspection reveals deep scratch marks on the inside of the lid, suggesting that something—or someone—was trapped inside and desperately trying to claw its way out.
