<Section icon={Target} title="Core Investigation Loop and Mechanics">

Solving the murder of Captain Magnus Mortuga requires more than just clicking on dialogue options. Players must master a repeatable investigation loop to uncover contradictions in the crew's testimony. The game is divided into chapters, beginning with "The Curse in the Cauldron," where you first establish the facts of the locked-room mystery.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Room Exploration">
    Enter new areas like the Illusion Theatre or the Dream Chamber. Use the cursor to find highlighted objects and environmental clues that hint at the submarine's secrets.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="3D Evidence Inspection">
    Once a clue is collected, rotate it 360 degrees. Look for hidden switches, labels, or damage on the base and interior of objects to register all inspection hotspots.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Crew Interrogation">
    Present your findings to the eight primary suspects. Each character has unique reactions to every piece of evidence, which are recorded in your notebook.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Deduction and Conclusion">
    Combine established testimony with physical proof in the deduction interface. Successfully linking a motive to a clue unlocks personal investigations and moves the story forward.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The "Deduction Interface" is the heart of the game's logic system. Instead of guessing, you must build sentences that describe a suspect's actions and then attach the specific item that proves it. If you fail, Grimoire and Sally provide contextual hints to guide you toward the correct logic.

| Tool | Function | Usage Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Case File** | Tracks main objectives | Review after every major cutscene |
| **Notebook** | Logs testimony and clues | Icons indicate unread character reactions |
| **Submarine Map** | Quick travel between floors | Check for new room unlocks after interviews |
| **Hint System** | Provides spoiler-free tips | No penalty for using multiple hint tiers |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Inspection Tip">
Many players get stuck because they haven't fully rotated a 3D object. Ensure you check the bottom and back of every item, especially mechanical ones like the Diving Helmet or the Broken Handheld Radio.
</Callout>

</Section>
