<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Solving the Mess Hall Cabinet Puzzle">

As you venture further into the submarine, you will encounter the Mess Hall, which features a massive metal statue of a sea serpent. Built into this statue is a locked cabinet containing a vital piece of evidence. The lock mechanism consists of four dials, each surrounded by small windows that display geometric shapes when turned.

The key to solving this puzzle lies in the relationship between the target numbers displayed in the center of the dials and the number of sides on the shapes appearing in the adjacent windows.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Examine the Serpent Statue">
    Locate the locked cabinet in the Mess Hall/Kitchen area and click on the dial mechanism to zoom in.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Analyze the Shapes">
    Rotate the dials to see which shapes (triangles, squares, pentagons) appear in the windows. Note that triangles represent 3, squares represent 4, and pentagons represent 5.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Match Sides to Numbers">
    Align the shapes so their collective side count matches the central dial numbers. For example, a dial displaying '3' requires a triangle in its window.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Claim the Sun Tablets">
    Once all four dials are correctly aligned, press the confirmation button to open the cabinet and retrieve the Sun Tablets.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Dynamic Hint System">
If you struggle with any puzzle, failing it three times unlocks progressive hints from Grimoire and Sally, detailing the puzzle mechanics without giving away the final solution.
</Callout>

The Sun Tablets clue is a highly detailed 3D bottle. When rotated, you can see individual tablets shaking inside the translucent glass. This bottle features a hand-painted sun emblem, which directly contrasts with evidence found later in the investigation.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Illusion Theater Crime Scene & Mind Palace Deduction">

The climax of the demo takes place in the Illusion Theater, a cramped, eerie corridor located at the very back of the submarine. This is the official crime scene where Captain Magnus Mortuga was discovered dead inside a locked room. 

The victim lies on his back with a deep cut across his neck and an ancient stone cauldron resting at his feet. Strangely, no murder weapon is present in the room, setting up a classic impossible crime scenario.

<Callout type="tip" title="Mind Game Logic">
Do not guess randomly. Read the connecting phrases aloud to ensure they flow logically. The game features full voice acting for incorrect sentences, which can sometimes offer subtle clues.
</Callout>

To solve this initial chapter, players must use the "Mind Game" deduction interface. This mechanic represents Detective Grimoire's mental process, requiring you to construct a logical sentence by selecting subjects, verbs, and objects from your gathered evidence.

| Evidence Item | Physical State | Narrative Clue | Analytical Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stone Cauldron | Open / contains crystal | Supposedly never unlocked before | Spin to find scratch marks inside |
| Broken Moon Vial | Spilled liquid on floor | Complements the Sun Tablets | Examine translucent glass bottom |
| Flipped Poster | Taped upside down | Magic show advertisement | Rotate to view sketch layout on back |
| Blood Trail | Pooled near neck | Leads toward the back of the sub | Follow path to analyze movement |

To successfully complete the deduction and transition into Chapter 1, construct the following sentence in the Mind Game interface: 

**"Magnus Mortuga unlocked the cauldron and was killed by what was inside."**

After submitting this deduction, the game prompts you to point out a physical detail on the stone cauldron. Rotate the cauldron to peer inside, select the scratch marks along the inner rim, and confirm your selection to complete the demo.

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Post-Game Content and Art Gallery">

Completing the demo grants players access to the Art Gallery, a special feature that remains fully unlocked in the final release once the credits roll. This gallery offers a fascinating look behind the scenes of the game's development, showcasing the transition from 2D sketches to 3D hand-painted models.

The gallery features extensive annotations by lead concept artist Katherine Anger and 3D artist Kindra. These notes detail early character designs for Captain Mortuga, environmental layouts for the Stone Top Inn, and the technical challenges of creating unlit, wobbly 3D models that perfectly mimic the aesthetic of 2D paintings.
