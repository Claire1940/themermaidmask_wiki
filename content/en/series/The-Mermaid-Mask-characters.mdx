<Section icon={Search} title="Investigation and Interrogation Mechanics">

Interacting with **The Mermaid Mask characters** requires more than just clicking through dialogue. You must use the evidence collected from the crime scene to challenge their statements. The game introduces a "succinct yet exhausted" rundown of operational philosophy, which players must parse for inconsistencies.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Questioning">
    Approach the suspect and ask basic questions regarding their whereabouts during the time of Magnus Martuga's death.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Presenting Evidence">
    Use items found on the floor of the crime scene to trigger specific reactions or memories from the character.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Identifying Contradictions">
    Compare the current statement with previous testimony. If a character lies about their relationship with Martuga, a "Breakthrough" occurs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Final Deduction">
    Link the character's motive to the specific method used to bypass the locked door of the submarine cabin.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The interrogation system is built on a foundation of academic certainty versus dreadful secrets. Use the following table to understand how different evidence types affect character interactions.

| Evidence Type | Effect on Dialogue | Best Used Against |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Personal Items** | Triggers emotional responses | Close associates of Magnus |
| **Technical Logs** | Provides hard data on movements | Engineers and Command staff |
| **Mysterious Artifacts** | Unlocks "Curse" related topics | Occult-leaning characters |
| **Blood Samples** | Forces a confession or alibi | Characters found near the scene |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Strategy">
When a character says they "couldn't say with any academic certainty," they are usually hiding a specific detail about a "solemn secret." Press them further using physical evidence.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="The Victim: Magnus Martuga">

Though he is deceased from the start, Magnus Martuga remains one of the most important **The Mermaid Mask characters**. His influence is felt in every corner of the submarine. To solve his murder, you must reconstruct his final hours and understand what kind of man he truly was.

Magnus was not just a captain; he was a man of "great will and great strength." His death has left a vacuum of power that Miss Seafoam has quickly filled. However, the pooling blood beneath him and the locked door suggest that his strength was not enough to protect him from an internal threat.

| Attribute | Description | Impact on Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reputation** | Fearsome and Unyielding | Created many enemies among the crew |
| **Final Action** | Locked himself in his room | Suggests he knew a threat was coming |
| **Last Seen** | Meeting an unknown individual | Identifies the window of opportunity |
| **Physical State** | Blood pooling beneath him | Indicates a violent, close-quarters struggle |

Understanding Magnus's "operational philosophy" is key to unlocking the submarine's restricted areas. Many of the puzzles in the game require you to think like Magnus to find his hidden stashes of evidence.

<Callout type="info" title="Lore Note">
The "Mermaid Mask" itself may be more than just a title; early clues suggest it is a physical object Magnus was guarding before his untimely death.
</Callout>
