<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Construction Process">

Building The Mermaid Mask creativebloq requires a methodical approach to ensure the structural integrity matches the visual complexity. Follow these refined steps to achieve a professional finish.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Base Template and Fitting">
    Begin by creating a duct tape dummy of the wearer's head to ensure a perfect fit. Transfer these dimensions to your EVA foam or thermoplastic base. The base should cover the upper third of the face while leaving the jaw free for movement.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Sculpting the Aquatic Features">
    Use lightweight clay or heat-shaped foam to build the brow ridges and cheekbone extensions. Focus on creating "flow lines" that lead the eye toward the back of the head, mimicking the streamlined shape of a swimming predator.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Scale Application">
    Apply individual scales starting from the bottom and working upward. For The Mermaid Mask creativebloq aesthetic, vary the scale sizes: larger on the forehead and progressively smaller near the temples.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Fin and Gill Integration">
    Attach the transparent fin elements made from Worbla or thin PETG. Use a heat gun to create organic ripples in the edges. These should be anchored firmly into the foam base using mechanical fasteners or high-strength epoxy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Sealing and Priming">
    Apply 3-4 thin coats of flexible primer. Sand lightly between coats with 400-grit sandpaper to remove any texture imperfections. This creates the "canvas" for your iridescent paint job.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Tip">
To create realistic "wet" scales, apply a final coat of high-gloss UV resin over individual scales rather than the whole mask. This creates a shimmering, multi-dimensional effect that catches the light during movement.
</Callout>

</Section>
