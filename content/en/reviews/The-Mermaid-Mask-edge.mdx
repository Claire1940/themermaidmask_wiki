<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Mermaid Mask edge: Core Investigation Mechanics">

To solve Captain Mortuga's locked-room murder with absolute precision, securing **The Mermaid Mask edge** is essential for every aspiring detective. The game combines traditional point-and-click mechanics with interactive three-dimensional evidence inspection. Unlike standard narrative adventures, progression relies heavily on your ability to manipulate physical objects, identify structural inconsistencies, and present the correct evidence during intense character interviews.

The core gameplay loop is structured around finding evidence and showing it to the right people. When you first enter a room, interact with every highlighted object. Some clues are simple documents, while others are fully modeled 3D objects. Rotating these objects through a full circle is crucial. Many critical markings, switches, or hidden compartments are located on the base, back, or inner lining of items like the Glass Eyeball or the broken handheld radio.

| Clue Category | Primary Focus | Investigation Impact | Priority Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| Document | Read both sides of pages, inspect handwriting | High | ★★★★★ |
| Physical Evidence | Rotate 360 degrees, check seams and dials | Critical | ★★★★★ |
| Environmental | Match visual symbols to mechanical locks | Medium | ★★★★☆ |
| Recording | Listen for conversational contradictions | High | ★★★★★ |

Once a clue is registered in your notebook, it becomes an active topic of conversation. The crew members aboard the Mortuga submarine are highly eccentric, and their reactions to physical evidence will change as the story progresses. To maintain your strategic advantage, you must exhaust every dialogue option. Ask each suspect about themselves, the victim, and every other crew member to build a comprehensive web of relationships and motives.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Always inspect the reverse side of paper documents. For example, the newspaper page found in the Child's Room contains an illusion story on the front, but the reverse side holds a critical sea-serpent report that unlocks dialogue with Madam Tadpole.
</Callout>

| Puzzle Name | Room Location | Solution Method | Reward / Unlock |
|---|---|---|---|

This unlocks the handwritten letter from Ibis to J.D. Wirman. In the Airlock Grotto, solve the diving helmet mechanism by rotating each pipe dial so the symbols leaving one junction match the symbols entering the next, creating a continuous, unbroken route.

<Callout type="success" title="Success Tip">
